Who Am I?

What is Dissociative Identity Disorder?
Dissociative Identity Disorder (previously known as multiple personality disorder) is a psychological disorder characterized by the appearance of two or more distinct personalities. DID is linked to a childhood plagued of physical, sexual, or emotional abuse and trauma.
Many of us have felt dissociation, the feeling of not being ourselves of detachment from reality like daydreaming or getting lost in what you are doing. DID is a severe form of detachment where the person lacks the connection and control of his or him self's thoughts, memories, actions, and sense of identity. Psychologists theorizes that DID is a coping mechanism from childhood trauma. The person detached from reality so he or she does not "feel" the experience because it is too violent, traumatic or painful to cope with.

Is it real?

Because DID is a psychological disorder, it is sometimes very hard to diagnose people that they have DID. The diagnosis still remains highly controversial among psychologists. Many health professionals say that DID is not real and is just Borderline personality disorder or just a coping method. The DSM-5, a manual containing classifications of psychological disorders, contains many more dissociative disorders, like dissociative amnesia and depersonalization. 

Do you have DID?
There are many tests online that help you decide that DID may be a possible diagnosis of your symptoms. A trained healthcare professional is required to get a diagnosis, and is recommended if you have any concerns regarding your health. Remember, there is always help available.
